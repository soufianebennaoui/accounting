Choose a trading company to set up your business

Commercial companies are grouped into two main families: single-member companies (SASU, EURL) and multi-member companies (SAS, SARL). It is necessary to balance the advantages and disadvantages of each according to social status and tax system to be sure to make the right choice!




Choose self-employed status

Starting your business as a micro-entrepreneur is an ideal solution, as it requires few formalities. An online declaration, possibly an entry in the Trade and Companies Register (RCS) or the Trades Directory (RM), and you can invoice.
Accounting is also simplified: you only have to keep an up-to-date book of expenses and receipts. You will pay your social security contributions each month or quarter, depending on the option chosen.
Small problem: you cannot deduct your expenses from your turnover. This is also capped depending on the nature of your activity (artisanal, commercial or liberal). You may prefer to turn to a sole proprietorship, which may be limited liability (EIRL) to ensure better protection of your personal assets.
