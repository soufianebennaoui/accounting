6 ideas for preparing an original candy bar for a wedding

The candy bar is a staple of any event or celebration . There is no doubt that we all love sweets and confectionery. Or at least that we feel like it at one time or another. This way of presenting them in a beautiful and decorated space makes them even more attractive. So if you are hosting a wedding , a candy bar to sweeten the afternoon or evening for your guests is a must-have on that special day . And kids and adults will love it too!

What to include in a wedding candy bar?

A candy bar is above all a kind of  free buffet of dragees, sweets and confectionery products. Which are placed on a separate table in a self-service style. They are therefore distributed in different containers and plates. So that each person can choose and eat what they want on the table. To fill the containers with succulent candy products that delight lovers. Order them from a renowned confectioner  like  SAUDIAL  who specializes in confectionery. The main element of a wedding candy bar .Must be an  original table with a unique decoration  that will surprise and attract the attention of guests. Another solution is to make a candy bar cart of different heights that you can decorate with ribbons or tulle. You should also  pay special attention to containers for  dragees and candies .

Must-Have Ideas for Creating a Wedding Candy Bar

The main items to include in a wedding confectionery are dragées and candies . That is why at SAUDIAL you will find a unique section of  dragees for weddings with different proposals for themes inspired by sweet tables, perfect to fit into the atmosphere chosen for that day. But if you want to have a special sweet space, here are some original ideas for your candy bar:

  • Focus on one theme or color to make it easier to choose the colors of the favors and treats. For example, a  vintage or rustic wedding confectionery   will offer treats in colors that are not too bright and muted.
  • Choose  several types of candies  and accompany them with other sweets such as cake pops , cookies, cupcakes or donuts.
  • Add salty foods to create contrast, such as popcorn, nuts, pretzels or saltine crackers.
  • In addition to placing treats on plates, make other more eye-catching items with them like skewers and candy cupcakes.
  • Add separate small bowls with tongs so guests can make their own assortment of treats instead of having to pick them one by one.
  • Prepare individual bags so they can also take home a treat with the sweets of their choice.
