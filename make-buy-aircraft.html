What to do before buying an airplane?

Are you tired of long queues at the airport or have you always dreamed of flying ? If so, you might be interested in  buying an airplane . But before you jump into the world of aircraft ownership , there are many factors you need to consider, including cost, maintenance, and more.

Obtain a license and prepare a budget

If you do not yet have a flight license, the first step is to  become a pilot , that is, to decide what type of license or pilot certificate you will need. Besides the famous  Private Pilot License , you can obtain a Recreational Pilot Certificate , which is less expensive than the Private Pilot License and takes less time to obtain. If you just intend to be a pilotoccasional or weekend pilot who departs for an hour or two alone or with another person in a single-engine propeller aircraft, then a  recreational or sport pilot certificate  may be sufficient.

But if you want to fly multiple people and fly longer distances, the private pilot license is the best option. However, you’ll need more than a private pilot ‘s license if you plan to fly a twin-engine propeller plane or jet…

Also, before buying an aircraft , you must  have a budget that  fits this purchase. Seeing what you can afford should be at the top of your list. Indeed, buying a plane without knowing how much you can afford can put you in serious debt or jeopardize your other financial investments. When determining how much you can afford, consider not only the  cost of purchasing the aircraft , new or used, but also the  costs of upkeep ,  maintenance , fuel ,  storage  and  insurance . Speaking of which, buying an aircraft from  Blueberry Aviation  may be your best bet if you are considering this option.

Start researching and testing before concluding a contract

When it comes to general aviation aircraft , your options are endless. There are propeller planes with one or two engines, as well as small jet planes . Even if you have an idea of ​​the type of plane you want to buy, you will need to see what is available and what is cheaper. And before buying the machine,  a test flight  should be your priority. If you’re not sure what to look for when considering an aircraft to buy, consider bringing a team of experts with you, such as an aircraft mechanic.

See if there are  any problem areas  that would prevent you from buying the device, ask for accident and incident reports for the type of device you are considering. And see if you can find complete folders for the device in question. Although a test flight may be one of your last stops, it’s good to make sure you’re  comfortable on the plane . before buying it. And once you’ve found the aircraft that’s right for you, it’s time to make your offer and anticipate the negotiations, get your title deed, and make sure you have aircraft insurance for your new one. purchase.
