Find out more about electrical accreditation training

Electrical safety in business is a matter of very high importance, given the number of risks associated with this area. It lies mainly in minimizing the number of accidents in each of the projects carried out, regardless of their size or execution. In order to maintain electrical safety, employers are required to train their employees. And give them an electrical authorization in accordance with the standards in force.

How important is electrical certification training?

Article R.4535-12 of the Labor Code, all workers working on a site exposed to high electrical risks. Must have an ample level of knowledge in the matter. Due to the regulations concerning the prevention of occupational risks, the training of workers is compulsory. In this sense, the law stipulates that the employer must ensure that each worker receives training in prevention when they are hired. During changes in the functions he performs or during the introduction of new technologies or changes in work equipment.

In the case of electrical risk , this training and information concerns workers who carry out operations on electrical installations . But it also concerns all workers who, due to their physical proximity to live installations. Or because they work in places presenting a risk of fire or explosion (in particular where there is a possibility of accumulation of static electricity ), they may be exposed to the risks generated by electricity. It is now possible to follow a elearning training electrical accreditations  for the theoretical part. It is also available on USB key/CD-Rom.

What type of training for which worker?

Companies must have qualified personnel and electrical protective equipment.   Certified suitable for performing correct tasks and training less experienced workers. In addition, certain guidelines must be followed to guarantee. That electrical safetyis ensured on a daily basis and that accidents are avoided. To this end, each employer must find the appropriate training for his employee, according to his activity and his missions. In order to establish the appropriate training for each worker, a distinction could be made between three different types of workers:

Users of electrical equipment and/or installations:

Training and information should be at a basic level, as simple and brief as possible. Expressed in easily understood terms, all depending on the experience and training of the workers concerned. Depending on the worker’s activity, emphasis should be placed on the risks likely to occur most frequently.

Non-electrical activity near electrical installations

In addition to the training and general information indicated in the previous section, adapted to the characteristics of the specific work they perform. Workers must be trained in the preventive measures to be taken to avoid invading the danger zone. On the collective protection and personal protective equipment (PPE) to be used, if applicable.

With regard to PPE, the worker must have sufficient information or training to know the characteristics of a given PPE, so as not to be exposed to situations for which the PPE does not offer any guarantee. .

Workers in maintenance and electrical installations

For these workers, in addition to the training indicated in the two previous sections. This must be much more extensive and, at the same time, very specific for each type of work to be carried out. Thanks to electrical accreditation training , they will be able to handle such and such systems.
