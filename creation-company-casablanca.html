Creation of a company

ALARIS LAW FIRM defines the creation of a company as « the implementation of new means of production ». It thus distinguishes the creation from the resumption of a company. Indeed, in this second hypothesis, you use already existing means of production to pursue an activity.
Behind ALARIS LAW FIRM‘s definition of business creation, which is very pragmatic, there are unavoidable steps and procedures, but also a way of life specific to the entrepreneur. Because creating a company means creating your own job, creating wealth and participating fully in the economy of a country!
Everything starts with a creation project, which must be transformed into a profitable and sustainable economic activity. You will have to formalize all these steps in a document called the business plan.
Finally, creating a company in Casablanca is a way of life, a permanent challenge, an ability to juggle with one’s cash flow and to make decisions whose consequences impact the standard of living of the entrepreneur and his employees.

What are the steps in creating a company?

1st step: Determine your business creation project

There are those who create their company by opportunity or because they have been carrying a project within them for some time. There are also those who are attracted by the entrepreneurial lifestyle and are looking for a great idea to exploit. If you fall into this second category, your first step in starting a business will be to find an idea and validate that there is a customer base willing to pay the price.

To find a business idea in line with current consumer trends (and with your aspirations), stay informed! Follow the media dedicated to new trends, get inspired by existing successful concepts…

Step 2: Conduct a market study to create your business

A good idea is not enough to join the world of entrepreneurship. All entrepreneurs will tell you that a market study is essential to determine if there is a clientele to buy your products or services. It also allows you to know the sector of activity concerned, the number of competitors and the relevance of your arrival on this market.

Finally, the market study is the starting point to deploy your penetration strategy, i.e. to find your positioning, your brand image, to set your prices, to set up your first range of products/services, an adequate distribution strategy… All these elements will then be written down in your business plan.

Step 3: Draw up a business plan for the creation or takeover of a company

The business creator must also think about a profitable business model. At this stage, you must carry out financial simulations until you build a reliable and profitable financial forecast. The economic part of your business project must also highlight the resources and partners that will be mobilized for financing, as well as your personal and professional budget in the future. The construction of the financial forecast in the business plan is generally carried out by a chartered accountant. This is an important moment in the life of the entrepreneur, as it formalizes the operating cycle of the future activity and shows the wealth that will be generated!

4th step: Choosing the legal status for the creation of the company

Creating a company aso means choosing a legal status and a tax system appropriate to the business model. The chartered accountant generally takes care of this step in the business plan.
From the micro-enterprise to the EURL, via the SASU and the individual company. The choice of the legal structure is essential because it conditions in particular :
The tax system to which your profits will be subject (income tax or corporation tax);
The social security system to which you will be attached (self-employed or salaried employee);
The extent of your liability in case of debts (limited or unlimited).
