Reasons to contact an accounting firm specialized in condominiums

As part of the management of a building subject to joint ownership, it is sometimes difficult to make decisions, especially when it comes to financial matters. So, co-owners are increasingly using  condominium accountants for the management of their buildings.  This solution has advantages in several respects. Indeed, apart from the management of economic issues, the chartered accountant of co-ownershipmay also provide other services to those who request it. These services cover, in particular, the administrative management of the property for which it is responsible. In the lines below, we present to you 3 of the services that you can benefit from by going to a condominium chartered accountant firm .

To keep accounts of your common good

It is important for co-owners to keep track of financial transactions on behalf of their common good. To do this, it is recommended to entrust this task to an expert in the field. The best way to contact them is to go to a  condominium accounting firm . When you contact a condominium accountant, you will no longer have to worry about keeping your property’s accounts. Indeed,  the accountant is responsible for most of the financial operations  :

  • the collection of rents from the property;
  • payment of suppliers;
  • monitoring of real estate transactions, etc.

To better administer the finances of the co-ownership

It sometimes happens that one or more  co -owners are unable to pay their dues. The co-ownership accountant may intervene at this level in order to manage pre-litigation with the latter. He will therefore be in charge  of debt collection . The accountant may for this purpose draw up a provisional budget , thanks to which the co -ownerscan follow a financial course of action. It will also be easier for the co -owners to have reserves of money to carry out work that they will have to do later.

Co – owners will be able to better anticipate expenses. They will then be able to optimize their financial capital. This is why the provisional budget is necessarily annexed during the general meetings of the co- owners. Thus, the latter will be able to see it and also give their approval.

To fulfill accounting and tax obligations

A co- ownership has rights, but also obligations to respect. These include accounting and tax obligations. To be sure of being in compliance with the standards, it is important to entrust the accounting management of the assets to an expert in the field. You will be able to contact expert real estate accountants who will accompany you step by step in all your  accounting and tax procedures . The accountant will therefore make the tax declarations on behalf of theco-owners.

It is important to know that certain accounting documents are required for better expense management and financial visibility. In addition, the condominium accountant may be responsible for making accounting entries in certain documents. Those are :

  • the general ledger in which he reports the transactions of the account;
  • the journal which brings together all the daily accounting operations ;
  • the trial balance in which is marked the summary of the balances of the accounts at the end of the financial year.
