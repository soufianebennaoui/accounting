What can a private detective do?

These investigators can work for all kinds of entities: individuals, lawyers, companies and more. If you are curious to know more about the figure and the work of this type of professional, continue reading.

When will you need a detective?

It often happens that the police or any other government agency has an excessive workload. So, it would be wise to recruit an agent who can  obtain evidence  to present to the authorities. By connecting to  astrategie.com/toulon , you will come across a  private detective firm  in the Var department. It is not always necessary for the policeare involved in a case. A private investigator can help solve serious crimes or misdemeanors, but also:

  • Check the career history of a candidate;
  • Acquire confirmations about infidelity;
  • Conduct investigations on economic issues, industrial property, etc.

His professional activity

The  work of the private detective  includes three areas:

Private: for example, a case of annoying neighbors against whom a person wants to gather evidence.

Company: A curious case could be that of a company which suspects that certain people make false declarations to the insurance companies .

Legal: for example, a private investigator can be hired by a spouse to find out, in the context of a divorce, the behavior of the other spouse towards the children. Especially in cases where child custody is at stake.

In all these missions, he will be able to perform several activities:

  • Observe and follow suspicious people, by car or on foot.
  • Go where the suspect is going, whether it’s a bar or a church.
  • Surveillance that may involve long hours of waiting in a discreet corner.
  • Clicher a suspect.

What area can it not investigate?

In principle, they do not interfere in fields which constitute derogations liable to prosecution. Also, they do not have the power to use in their investigations  material or technical devices  that  affect personal life and the right to honor . In other words, the most important thing to keep in mind is that a private  investigator cannot interfere in cases which, by their nature, are tried in court . On rare occasions, a private investigator can collaborate with the justice system, provided the judge authorizes it.

What are its types of customers?

This professional can collaborate with  any natural or legal person  wishing to prove his suspicions about  another person ,  organization  or  company . The information must be legally demonstrable and may have different purposes. Out of curiosity, the legal experts who most often use the services of a private investigator are  lawyers specializing in family law., in matters of custody, pensions, inheritance, guardianship of minors or domestic violence.
